# Audio Signal Separation using FFT-Based Digital Filtering

## Overview

This project focuses on separating vocal and instrumental components from an audio recording using MATLAB. The implementation uses Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) and digital filtering techniques to analyze the frequency spectrum and isolate different components of the audio signal.

## Objectives

* Analyze audio signals in the frequency domain.
* Identify frequency ranges corresponding to vocals and instruments.
* Design digital filters for signal separation.
* Reconstruct separated signals for further analysis.

## Methodology

1. Load and normalize the audio signal.
2. Compute the FFT to obtain the frequency spectrum.
3. Design a Butterworth high-pass filter.
4. Separate instrumental and vocal components.
5. Perform inverse processing to reconstruct signals.
6. Visualize time-domain and frequency-domain responses.

## Technologies Used

* MATLAB
* Fast Fourier Transform (FFT)
* Digital Signal Processing
* Butterworth Filter Design

## Results

* Successful separation of vocal and instrumental signals.
* Frequency-domain visualization of original and processed signals.
* Comparative analysis of filtered outputs.

## Applications

* Music production
* Audio restoration
* Speech enhancement
* Signal processing research
